About

The main challenge in any manufacturing process is the gap between theory and practice. It's that moment when laboratory methods don't scale to large volumes, standard equipment can't handle the task, and bringing in a dozen different contractors only leads to missed launch deadlines and equipment breakdowns.

By acting as a single point of responsibility, I manage the entire production cycle of your turnkey project: from adapting methods to available raw materials and the required volume to assembling custom reactors or equipping the line with ready-made equipment from my own warehouse. By entrusting the launch of your line to a single specialist, you eliminate financial losses between project phases and receive a system in which the chemical formula and equipment engineering are perfectly synchronized with each other.

Whether it involves overcoming a technological bottleneck, scaling up production, fulfilling a complex custom request, or providing expert consultation — the result of our collaboration is always cost-effective and uninterrupted production that consistently delivers the right product in the right quantity.

Scale-Up

About this service

Attempting to apply laboratory ratios directly to industrial volumes will inevitably disrupt the process. This leads to uncontrolled reactions, high defect rates, and a loss of product quality.

To avoid financial losses during scale-up, it is essential to adapt your chemical synthesis to new production facilities with mathematical precision. Conducting a thorough engineering recalculation of hydrodynamics, heat transfer, and mass transfer allows the core methodology to be fully adapted to the actual physics of large-scale reactors.

You receive calibrated temperature profiles, precise dosing algorithms, and mixing parameters that guarantee process stability, even when working with complex or viscous media. This approach completely eliminates the need for trial-and-error with expensive raw materials: every stage of the transition is simulated and calculated in advance.

The result is a robust industrial process that allows you to confidently produce large batches while maintaining the benchmark quality of the original sample.
